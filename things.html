Things to do or visit:

To think of it, when the city Buffalo comes up in discussions the exclamation that pops up, swift, is: “It is too cold”. Facts accepted. But there is more to Buffalo than #cold #winter #snow! Every place brings with itself a palette of attractions unique to itself, this place has its own charm as well.

Not called for a mention, the majestic, awe-inspiring, signature Niagara Falls is the star attraction at Buffalo. Be it the warm sunshine or the frosty icicles this spot is an eye-candy throughout the year. The “Maid of the mist” and “Cave of the winds” are one-of-a-kind of experiences that should be indulged in without a doubt.

There is more to Buffalo, beyond the showstopper falls:

  • Letchworth State Park: Autumn comes with hues of colors. A visit to Letchworth is evidence of this. A delight for nature lovers, hiking enthusiasts and photographers; but worth a visit for everyone who is here to explore. Be it hiking trails or camping sites/cabins, it is an experience of a kind. A half mile trail takes you to the Lower falls, further ahead are the popular Middle Falls. Half a mile from the Middle Falls come the Upper Falls, watch out for the iron bridge (an active train trestle) that gauges across the falls. For adventure enthusiasts, river rafting is sought-after in summers.
  • Canalside in downtown Buffalo: Once the western terminus of the Erie Canal, is a popular attraction for various events throughout the year. The Lake Erie cradles a scenic waterfront that caters to events and concerts (many free ones too!), being a key attraction. On the contrary, if you have a fetish for history, this is the place where WWII warships are anchored for tours. In winters, it is the largest outdoor ice rink that opens for ice skating. And summers let you enjoy biking, canoeing, kayaking or just watching the sunset at the Erie Marina.
  • The Buffalo Zoo: Who says zoo visits are for kids? The Buffalo zoo is one of the busiest attractions in Western New York after the coveted Niagara Falls. It exhibits a vivid collection of wildlife and plant life. With an entry fee of $5 in January and February, it is an affordable attraction for all age groups. Watch out for the red pandas and the sea lions!
  • Ski resorts: What is Buffalo famous for? Yes, you are right, snow! Venture to the south of Buffalo, to experience skiing on the slopes at Holiday Valley, Kissing Bridge, Holimont, Buffalo Ski Club. Worried about being a newbie? Most ski resorts provide beginner lessons and gear rentals along with the lift pass. For discounts, you can consider UB’s Schussmeisters Ski Club.
  • Culinery Delights: Visit the Larkin Square to explore dozens of Food Trucks, all through summer and fall. From pizza platters,crepes, bbq meat platters to waffles, smoothies and much more; there something for every foodie. For restaurant experiences, don’t miss the world famous Buffalo wild wings; walk into the Anchor Bar to relish the dish. Also, the local restaurant week comes with attractive deals to enjoy various cuisines! . Tip: Try the sponge candy at Perry’s ice cream, it is a local favourite!
  • Sporting Events: When in Buffalo, live like a local! The city soaks in the fervor for various sports, the popular ones being American football, Ice Hockey, Baseball, Lacrosse. You can catch a game of the Buffalo Bills- the local American football team, at the New Era field south of Buffalo.
  • Arts and Theatre: There is something for everyone at Buffalo. Art lovers can explore the Albright-Knox Art Gallery that has a collection of rich exhibits. For those who look for a Broadway kind of experience can visit the Shea’s performing arts center located on Main street, downtown Buffalo. It hosts Broadway troupes, silent films and much more.
  • Buffalo City Hall: The architecture of this building stands out with intricate geometric details. But, more than its structural design, it is famous for the view that it offers, overlooking downtown Buffalo. The 25th floor offers an observation deck that gives you a spectacular view of Lake Erie and the city below.
  • Village of Lewiston:It is town located in Niagara county, common for its natural beauty and historic appeal. A visit to the Niagara Power project, is a key attraction. You can see how hydroelectricity is harnessed.
  • Buffalo and Erie county botanical gardens: Hosts a vivid collection of plant life. Designed by the designer of New York’s famous Central Park, this place caters to multiple exhibitions, educational programs and other events throughout the year.
  • Buffalo transportation Pierce-Arrow Museum: Fond of automobiles? Vintage cars? This is the place you ought to pay a visit. Located on the Seneca street, this museum is home to a memorable collection of vintage vehicles and historic artifacts. There is a guided tours facility through the museum to let know the et al of these majestic pieces.
  • Elmwood Village: On bright sunny day, a walk along the streets of Elmwood is good pick. It has some historic buildings, local/brand stores to shop from and of course some local restaurants to enjoy a meal. This neighborhood is mostly a shopper’s paradise
  • Buffalo River History tours: A 90-minute tour along the Buffalo river, aboard the Buffalo river history tour cruise tells you the story of the Erie Canal. This 48-seater River Queen takes you along the huge standing grain elevators, you can as well admire the beautiful city buildings through the tour.
  • Buffalo’s Delaware ParkLooking for a place outdoors to enjoy the weather and some fun time with friends, this is Buffalo’s Central Park. A good spot for pleasant strolls, picnics, includes a collection of gardens, patches of forests and lakes. One scenic place to spend a day out and relax. Tip: Look out for featuring Shakespeare works presented by the theatre company on a Grand Tudor-style stage!
  • Nightlife and entertainment:There are night spots all over the town, spreading across Allen street, Elmwood Avenue, University Heights and more. Some food and good music, calls for the SoHo Burger Bar; to dance the night away head straight to 67 West. There are bands that host free concerts periodically; a good bet to explore.
