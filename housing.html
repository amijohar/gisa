Utilities: What you should know?

While renting any apartment, pay attention to the additional costs that come with the rent. Some landlords include the utility bill in the rent itself, hence saves you all the payment hassle. But if in case the utility bill is renter's responsibility, the renter should know what they are getting into it or they might end up being exploited. The following are the average utility bill per month as per 2017:

  • Electricity: $50 depending on usage
  • Heating: $100 - $120 during winters depending on Usage, $50 during summer {subject to size of the house}
  • Internet: $60 - $70 (Verizon, Time Warner)
  • Water: $50
Dos:
  • Credit the rent payment in an escrow account which will be released only if habitability is met (better option) if not satisfied with the house.
    • Landlord fraud- Landlord providing misleading information-Occasions like showing fake pictures of the place and making students sign lease.
      • Need to contact Consumer Fraud Bureau
      • Under the office of New York State Attorney General’s office
    • Landlord- Tenant money issues
      • Need to go to Small claims court ( Under $5K)
      • No lawyer needed
    • If lawyer help needed to review lease agreement before signing
      • VLP Buffalo- Free
      • SBA – Free
    • Grass cutting/ Snow Removal
      • If tenant agreed to do- They have to do it, no way out unless they talk to the landlord and come to a different agreement(Need to be verified while signing the lease agreement)
      • If not done, city will fine the landlord and he can charge that fine + the cost of doing to the tenant
      • If landlord agreed to do- They have to do it. If not done, tenants can complain to the city of Buffalo and they will take action.
      • Always read the agreement and negotiate the terms with landlord
    Don’ts:
    • Do not sign the lease without visually inspecting the house.
    • Never say you won’t pay the rent if not satisfied with the house.
    Need to know information:
    • Once a lease is signed, tenant has 3 days to back out(Cancel) of the lease
      • University heights collaborative
        • Meets on 2nd Tuesday of every month at glory parks
        • Tenants has the right to take action if warrants of habitability is not met (Water, Heat, Gas, Electric, Roof, Insulation)
      • Call 311 for building inspectors
        • Should not sound like tenant- landlord issue(When talking to them, do not mention about the landlord not returning the security deposit ,etc.)
        • Tenant has to be willing to let the inspector inside the house
