Brace Yourself: Winter is Coming

If you’re coming to UB, you’ve probably heard of how crazy the weather here can get. *Insert GoT theme music*. If you haven’t, Buffalo receives more snowfall than most other cities in the USA, and has average low temperatures of about -7 degree Celsius. With proper clothing and preparation, however, winter can be more like a white fairyland and less like hell frozen over.

Here are some tips we have found useful to survive through those long buffalo winters.

1. For starters, get a GOOD winter coat. You can skimp on most other things, but it is highly advisable to get a sturdy, waterproof coat that can last you a long time- it’s an investment. Don’t buy your winter clothing in India, wait till you come here. North Face, Hollister and Eddy Bauer are some well-known brands here. Once you arrive here, go to a local store like Kohl’s, Target or Macy’s, preferably with a person who has spent a winter in Buffalo for a good-fitting and warm coat you find comfortable. You should be spending 50 to 130 $.

Note: Fall is reasonably chilly, but not cold enough to wear a winter jacket. You might want a Fall jacket (25 to 50 $), or just layer up with a long sleeved tee/shirt, a sweater/sweatshirt/both, and a scarf.

2. Don’t forget to get a pair of waterproof and warm boots, fuzzy/warm socks, sweaters/sweatshirts, scarf, 2 pairs of gloves, a hat, fleece leggings (for girls) and thermal wear (optional). Since the classrooms are warm, you might be hot if you wear thermal wear you can’t remove. The socks, gloves and hat are very important. Again, shop locally, if possible.

3. Wear layers- If you need thermal wear, try layering with sweaters or sweatshirts instead. Pro tip: Even if you have the warmest winter jacket ever, you can’t stay warm without layers. Layers are key.

4. If you’re renting/leasing a house/apartment, make SURE you’re getting one with good heating. Don’t skimp on rent by freezing to death in the winter.

5. Nights are cold, and no matter how warm your living space, you will need a comforter (A comforter is a stuffed, super-warm, comfy quilt). You should be able to find one between 15 and 50 $.

As soon as you arrive, learn to get from one building to the other without getting outside (This is very useful during the winters as you will not have to wear your jacket, gloves and a million other things to go to the next building). Here’s an article about UB’s ‘tunnels’: https://goo.gl/xB67hP

Here’s to a super warm and fun stay at Buffalo! Hope you love it as much as we do!
